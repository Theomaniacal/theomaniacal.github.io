Programming and Development

One of my more recent hobbies has been programming. Starting with an interest in game development, I learned the language Lua. Recently in September, I entered the Electronic Game Developers Society Game Jam 2014. To my pleasant surprise, my team won best in show! With this motivation, I am now working on bigger projects. In order to better my game development skills, I have also been reading on game design. It's a fascinating field that actually relates a lot to marketing analysis. A game designer's main job is to create a game that is able to provide a dynamic experience from multiple lenses. Similarly, a marketing analyst works to find a product that will perform well in a market by ensuring the product creates positive experiences within multiple consumers.

I'm also learning to become better at web development (HTML, CSS, Javascript) along with other practical languages such as Python.
