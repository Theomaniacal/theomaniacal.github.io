Why Economics?

Starting since high school, I constantly noticed the marketability of foods in grocery stores. One of the first trends I saw was that bigger companies' products more frequently occupied the ends of the store aisles. This led me to become more interested in how companies try to influence a consumer's choice. The question, "What key factors influence a certain product's demand?" brought me into the world of microeconomics.

Why Psychology?

Going beyond consumer choice, my interest in people expanded into a broader field. To understand how attractive a product is, we must first understand the consumer. Positive psychology is a branch that focuses on the happy aspects of life, rather than focusing on negative phenomena such as mental illness. With this knowledge, we can discover which goods and services to produce and sell based off of what makes people satisfied and happy.
